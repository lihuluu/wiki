Most of us are familiar with Middle Eastern dips like hummus and baba ganoush, but one that’s often conspicuously absent from party spreads is labneh.

Labneh’s relative scarcity from American tables is befuddling. The creamy spread, made from yogurt strained even further than the more familiar Greek variant, is similar in texture and flavor to soft cheese. Labneh is delicious in its basic form but is also an excellent base for bringing in additional spices and flavors.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Bezi_all.png?w=3000&quality=65&ssl=1)

[Bezi](http://eatbezi.com/) is a new brand seeking to introduce consumers to the snacking possibilities of labneh. The branding identity, designed by [Red Antler](https://www.redantler.com/), certainly helps labneh make an excellent first impression. The look is fun, and the presentation appeals to millennials and Gen Z consumers.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Frame-6-1-scaled.jpg?w=2048&quality=70&ssl=1)

“Our objective from the start was clear: to unseat hummus as the go-to healthy(ish) snack food. We aimed for Bezi to become a mainstay for everyday snacking and Super Bowl parties alike,” said Rohan Krishnan, principal strategist at Red Antler. “Whether it’s spread on toast, served as a dip, or even used as a base for dressings and sauces, Bezi is about bringing this traditional Middle Eastern staple into the modern kitchen. We wanted to align the versatility of Bezi’s tang-free recipe with the wildly inventive ways we snack, eat, and share foods with the people in our lives.”

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/3_Bezi-Product-scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C1137&quality=70&ssl=1)

When crafting a brand identity, it’s easy to lean too much into a product’s cultural origins, but Bezi strikes the right balance. Visually, the brand highlights the yummy qualities first through pleasant illustrations of creamy labneh, a ruddy whole bell pepper, or a sliced everything bagel. The top-notch typography invites the curious foodie to pick up the tub and discover something new.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Frame-7-scaled.jpg?w=2048&quality=70&ssl=1)

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Bez-Bubbles.png?resize=2048%2C1017&quality=65&ssl=1)

Regardless, Bezi’s brand identity isn’t overtly Middle Eastern, but it still connects to labneh’s roots. “‘Bezi’ is a coined word derived from ‘bez,’ the Turkish word for ‘cloth.’ This name nods to the traditional use of cheesecloths in labneh making while still feeling distinctively modern,” Rohan explains.

Even though most people don’t think about labneh as a dip or spread option before other Middle Eastern mainstays like hummus, Bezi’s attractive brand identity is appealing and contemporary enough to change that, inviting consumers to add to their snacking repertoire.
