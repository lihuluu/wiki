From the Emmy award-winning series *The Bear* to *New York Times* best-selling cookbooks, Matty Matheson has made a name for himself as a culinary legend who knows how to add a whole lot of flavor to whatever he cooks. Now, with [Matheson Food Company](https://www.mathesonfoodcompany.com/), he’s serving his bold and punchy tastes to pantries across Canada.

To translate his vision into a timeless brand with bold shelf appeal, Matheson worked with design agency [Wedge](https://www.wedge.work/) to design memorable and dynamic packaging that authentically reflects the celeb chef.

![1-wedge-mfc-overview.png](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/df2ba5fb-ac2a-43ca-b464-9693dcf47305_1wedgemfcoverview.png?w=2373&quality=65&ssl=1)

Whether it’s sauces, dressings, or comfort foods like mac and cheese, Matheson aims to infuse every dish with his unmistakable signature flavors and the memories of this youth. “The brand and packaging design is directly inspired by one of Matty’s favorite childhood foods: Hereford Corned Beef. The custom logomark is a direct wink,” explains Justin Lortie, co-founder and design director of Wedge. “We embraced the straightforward, practical, graphic, no-fuss approach, primary colors, and vintage vernacular that runs through Matty’s cookbooks, merch, and world.”

The packaging design is simple yet striking, with bold primary colors and a vintage-inspired aesthetic that reflects Matty’s personality and culinary style. It’s a nod to the no-fuss approach that runs through his overall brand. “In Matty’s words, ‘simple as possible.’ Red, Yellow, Blue, Green. All products carry the red wordmark, stamped like the official mark of approval,” notes Lortie.

![4-wedge-mfc-bbq.png](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/37876232-2e60-4f00-a9d1-577013edcb09_4wedgemfcbbq.png?w=2233&quality=65&ssl=1)

One of the challenges of designing the packaging was balancing Matheson’s persona with the need to appeal to a wide demographic. “MFC has Matty’s name in it; it’s obviously ‘Matty approved,’” says Lortie. “But the idea was to create a company that goes beyond the character and transcends the man.” The result is a packaging system that connects with consumers by focusing on quality and flavor rather than just the celebrity behind it.

![5-wedge-mfc-shelf.png](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/7d557339-ad18-40f7-82e7-054412f4e4bd_5wedgemfcshelf.png?w=2430&quality=65&ssl=1)

The initial lineup includes BBQ sauces, salad dressings, and mac and cheese. Each product pays homage to his personal anecdotes and childhood memories, with flavors like Original BBQ Sauce, inspired by his dad’s recipe, and Green Olive Dressing, an ode to his mother-in-law Carol. The most exciting addition to the Matheson Food Company lineup is the soon-to-be-released Burn Your Tongue Caesar dressing. Matty describes it as “fire with lettuce and meats.”

With Matty Matheson at the helm, this brand will almost assuredly push boundaries and delight taste buds. In the end, it’s not just about bold flavors—it’s about bold design, too. With simple yet striking packaging, Matheson Food Company notably stands out on the shelves and promises to bring extra love into home cooking, one Matheson-inspired bite at a time.
