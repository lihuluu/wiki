*Wild Body Wash 2.0, a first of its kind plastic free and refillable body wash. Designed by Morrama, the refill simply drops into the case, the top screws on and it’s ready to go. The refill is made from Vivomer, a material that safely breaks down in soil and marine environments in as little as 6 months. The case itself is made from more than 50% recycled plastic and infinitely recyclable aluminum.*

*On average, British adults dispose of 312 plastic bottles each year, yet only half of us recycle bathroom packaging, often because it’s unclear whether it can be recycled. Founded in 2019, Wild has made it their mission to eliminate plastic from our bathrooms. The challenge for Morrama was how to create a fully biodegradable refill system that could still contain liquid effectively.*

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Wild-refill-body-wash_biodegradable--scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C1366&quality=70&ssl=1)

*Morrama has been working with Wild since 2019 and helping to develop and launch three products to date, including their hero refillable deodorant.*

*This project brought together three key players: Wild, the visionary client; Shellworks, the innovative partner behind the groundbreaking Vivomer material; and Morrama, the design experts who engineered and refined the product experience. Together, we created a solution that is functional, beautiful, and planet-conscious.*

*Most refill systems typically come in the form of a pour-in solution, such as multi-serve pouches or concentrate formats. While pouches can significantly reduce the amount of plastic used, they are often made from mixed materials that are impossible to recycle. Additionally, these pour-in solutions can be messy, and challenging to store. Wild was determined not to settle for simply reducing plastic by a certain percentage; the goal was to create a solution completely free of traditional plastic that also offered a seamless user experience.*

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Wild-refill-body-wash_design-process-scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C1366&quality=70&ssl=1)

*The original Wild body wash launched in 2023; a proof of concept that flew off the shelves and highlighted some of the key areas for improvement. We immediately set to work on version 2.0, streamlining the user experience (the first iteration had a separate pump that added an extra assembly step) and adopting the softer design aesthetic that had been applied to the updated new deodorant case.*

*One key challenge was ensuring both backward and forward compatibility. New refills had to fit into old cases, and old refills had to work with new cases. It was essential that users wouldn’t need to dispose of their existing cases to use the new refills. Achieving this required a close collaboration between all parties involved, clear communication, detailed technical drawings, and extensive testing.*

*Another challenge was creating a drop-in loading experience that would seal securely from the top while remaining compatible with two refill suppliers, each with different manufacturing tolerances and processes. This required close coordination and cooperation with all three factories to ensure the design worked seamlessly across the board.*

*Keeping the design compatible with multiple refill options ensures that Wild can remain agile, consistently iterating on the refill packaging to ensure they are putting the planet first without being restricted by legacy design work.*

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Wild-refill-body-wash_compostable-scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C1366&quality=70&ssl=1)

*The search for a plastic-free bottle began in 2021. Since then, Wild and Morrama have collaborated with numerous suppliers, dedicating countless hours to developing, testing, and eliminating different materials before launching their Bamboo-O packaging in 2023. While the original bamboo-based packaging worked well at a smaller scale, as Wild scaled up their body wash line, the team struggled to make the material work at mass production scale.*

*In response, Wild switched to using compostable refills made from Vivomer, a material developed by Shellworks. What makes Vivomer special is its ability to fully biodegrade in compost or marine environments without leaving microplastics behind. Despite this, it remains highly stable in use. It offers excellent barrier properties, making it ideal for holding liquids, and can be molded like traditional plastics, making it lightweight, versatile, and a simple, drop-in alternative to plastic packaging.*

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Wild-refill-body-wash_refill-in-hand-scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C2048&quality=70&ssl=1)

*An additional benefit is that Shellworks is based in the UK, which allows Wild to keep both development and manufacturing onshore. This enhances supply chain agility, reduces shipping times and costs, and minimizes environmental impact.*

*We’re fortunate to collaborate with a brand that shares our values and has the drive and resources to push the boundaries of innovative materials in the personal care space. However, working with new materials comes with its risks. Timelines can shift, and costs can creep up during development cycles, so it’s not for everyone. But if you’re willing to roll up your sleeves and get stuck in, you have the opportunity to create something truly new and impactful.*

*Following the launch of the body wash, the Wild team has expanded their range to include additional personal care products in the same innovative packaging such as shampoo and conditioner. With another new product set to launch later in 2025, we’re excited about the potential to further disrupt the personal care industry and remove plastic waste from the bathroom.*
