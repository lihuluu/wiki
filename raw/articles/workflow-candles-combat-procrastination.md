Workflow’s anti-procrastination candle packaging, designed by [Oleg FireSPX](https://www.behance.net/firespx), leans into precision. Each box is labeled with the burn time in bold, oversized type that feels almost like a timestamp.

The timer graphic runs along the top edge and reinforces a clockwatcher’s aesthetic, while the black-and-white palette keeps it sharp and focused. It’s super stripped back but purposeful, designed to help you stay on task. Even the copy reads like a product manual, down to the candle count and scent.

---

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/120d7d202249859.668304b095cda-scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C1152&quality=70&ssl=1)

*Workflow is a series of aromatic candles aimed at combating procrastination. There are 10 mini-candles per ceramic jar, each candle taking a certain amount of time to burn out, helping you to concentrate on work while it lasts — like a timer.*

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/706483202249859.668304b09923d-scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C1152&quality=70&ssl=1)

*Lighting a candle becomes a tradition that allows you to move the process away from a clean slate, and the series is divided into two aroma categories. The “calm down” lineup with soothing aromas represents the night and is designed to decrease hyperactivity and stimulate the ability to focus, while the “wake up” set is daylight, setting you up for work and filling with energy.*
