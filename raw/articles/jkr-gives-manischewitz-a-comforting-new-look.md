Kosher food brand Manischewitz has been around for over 130 years, slinging traditional Jewish food like matzo, macaroons, egg noodles, gefilte fish, and cake mixes, and they’ve remained a mainstay of the kosher aisle that sits alongside the likes of Streits and Yehuda.

But, according to them, that’s a big problem for the brand, and they wanted to appeal to a new generation of folks and break free of the kosher aisle.

![01_MANIZ_STILLS_KOP_LOGO.jpg](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/359f325e-f1dd-4b1e-9a80-955579b3153b_01_MANIZ_STILLS_KOP_LOGO.jpg?quality=70&ssl=1)

“Manischewitz is well-known amongst our loyal consumers who buy the brand day in, day out. In an effort to update the cultural relevancy with a younger Jewish audience as well as mainstream culturally curious audience, we have refreshed our brand with an exciting new look and feel on our current and new product offerings,” said Shani Seidman, CMO of Kayco, the parent company for Manischewitz, in a press release. “Manischewitz is not just about food; it’s about stories, heritage, and a sense of belonging. Through this rebranding, we aim to capture the hearts of the culturally curious and kosher-keeping alike, offering a taste of Jewish tradition that’s accessible to all.”

To update and modernize the brand, Manischewitz turned to JKR for a redesign that looks to its heritage but gives it a playful, comforting sheen that feels like a genuine overhaul. Of course, Manischewitz invited the agency to root through their archives and give them the usual factory tour. But JKR’s fundamental approach rested on exploring brands that have broken free of the ethnic aisle.

“Manischewitz holds a special place not only in Jewish culture, but American culture. Simply mentioning the brand evokes thoughts of Jewish cuisine, although people may not have had a clear visual of what it looked like. This presented an opportunity to establish a distinct brand identity that not only extends the understanding of Jewish culture through food but also beckons the mass-market consumer base to embrace a new category that goes beyond the confines of the Jewish food section,” explained Lisa Smith, global executive creative director, at JKR, in a press release. “At the same time, we wanted to respect and bring joy to Manischewitz’s loyal consumer base by enhancing their brand experience across all touchpoints.”

So let’s get the big things out of the way. Overall, the entire system has a warm, inviting feeling that leans into Manischewitz’s enduring reputation as a purveyor of pure comfort food—that’s matzo ball soup right there, kids. A warm color palette stretched across the brand world traffics in reds, yellows, and browns, making for quite the about-face. What’s more the typographic system comes to life as the logo gets freed from its badge. No more does Manischewitz sit on a straight line either; it arches ever-so-slightly, almost like your trusty neighborhood deli (though, yes, it can sit perfectly horizontal as the whole system has flexibility for days, or, for the sake of a redesign, *years*).

Illustrations also come into play and lend the brand and Jewish cuisine some comforting credence. Done up similar to *New Yorker* illustrations—where you maybe get the joke if there actually *was* a joke, to begin with—characters hold up plates of latkes or savor a bowl of soup. Everything is food, and your bubby won’t take no for an answer.

![05_MANIZ_STILLS_B&A_PACK.jpg](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/0b69ae70-445c-4b3b-b917-190c746e9a86_05_MANIZ_STILLS_B%26A_PACK.jpg?quality=70&ssl=1)

Take the update on the matzo box, for example. Where there used to be a blue and white box of matzo with some delectable photography, you’ll now find a welcoming orange alongside those characterful illustrations (in this case, a happy baker firing up some matzo in the oven). What’s more, the matzo itself becomes a patterned border on the box, an underrated detail that feels remarkably effective as it gives the entire box added texture.

![09_MANIZ_STILLS_POSTERS.jpg](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/df50874d-8e1e-4e67-b7fb-a1a33061a97b_09_MANIZ_STILLS_POSTERS.jpg?quality=70&ssl=1)

Overall, JKR loads the refresh with plenty of personality and what they call “craveable comfort.” They even introduce a verbal identity that they claim is equal parts candid Bubby, Seinfeld, and TikTok foodie, with plenty of matzo ball jokes and references to the celebratory quality of the food itself.

The revamped products should make their way to shelves before Passover on April 22nd, although I’ve already spotted the new Manischewitz designs here at my local grocery store in Massachusetts, so maybe you will, too.
