We all know Graza for its squeeze bottles.

Now, announced today, the brand is introducing a refill option featuring aluminum cans alongside its traditional plastic squeeze bottle format. This new approach, with design from [Gander](https://www.takeagander.com/), addresses the need for a more sustainable option and helps ensure the oil’s freshness and quality.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/06-Graza-Beer-Cans-Drizzle-Counter-scaled.jpg?w=2048&quality=70&ssl=1)

Yes, this is something Graza fans were clamoring for, but the brand saw it as a testament to its unwavering commitment to sustainability. Andrew Benin, Graza CEO and co-founder, underscores the popularity and recyclability of aluminum cans, making them a natural fit for our brand. “We didn’t just respond to consumer demand for refills; we took it a step further while ensuring we maintain our commitment to sustainability. We also tested the cans in food service settings to see how refilling at a high pace looked like, and the results were impressive.”

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/10-Graza-Beer-Cans-Please-Recycle.jpeg?resize=1972%2C2048&quality=70&ssl=1)

Benin explains that while a refill option was essential for their squeeze bottles, traditional large-bulk containers posed challenges due to the risk of rapid oil oxidation. “The beer can format maintains the integrity of the EVOO, as they are completely opaque and can be nitrogen-sealed for maximum freshness,” notes Benin. “Additionally, the beer cans are made from 100% recyclable aluminum, offering consumers a sustainable option for ordering more Graza Olive Oil without worrying about plastic waste. Beer cans are also lightweight, meaning they are cheaper to ship, keeping costs as low as possible for our customers.”

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/09-Graza-Beer-Cans-Pour.gif?resize=819%2C1024&ssl=1)

When asked if there were any challenges with the introduction of the cans, Benin noted that canning olive oil is much more difficult than canning a beverage. “Beverage brands can ‘overfill’ and then pressure wash their cans to remove all excess liquids. We can’t. There is lots of machinery engineering over here,” he mentions.

Regarding design, Graza embraced a departure from its traditional illustrations, drawing inspiration from the cultural resonance of the craft beer world. “A big consideration was whether or not to depart from our core sizzle and drizzle illustrations that have become synonymous with Graza. We mustered up the courage to do so and leaned into some of the cultural resonance in the “Beer world.” For example, the beer helmet character on Sizzle Refill Can” shares Benin.

“Another key challenge is betting on our packaging to make it clear that you need to use a funnel, which we find is a critical kitchen tool to have in the first place. We address that by launching our starter kit, where we partnered with OXO to include the perfect-sized funnel for our online customers.”

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/02-Graza-Dieline-Sizzle-Can-Detail-V2-scaled.jpg?resize=2048%2C1521&quality=70&ssl=1)

While the aluminum cans are not available in larger sizes, Graza remains committed to quality over quantity. They prioritize oil preservation and freshness by maintaining a 1:1 ratio with their squeeze bottles. Benin says, “Large olive oil formats only serve environments that demolish quantities of EVOO, like restaurants. The larger the vessel, the more risk there is that once oxidation begins, it impacts a larger amount of Olive Oil.” The cans will be available in retail, specifically Whole Foods Market stores nationwide, and direct-to-consumer via the Graza website.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/04-Graza-Beer-Cans-Pantry.gif?resize=820%2C1024&ssl=1)

Early feedback on the aluminum can packaging has been overwhelmingly positive, a testament to consumer appreciation for sustainable alternatives. Launching today, Graza invites consumers to join us in shaping a more sustainable future, one refill at a time, with its high-quality (and immensely welcomed) aluminum cans.
