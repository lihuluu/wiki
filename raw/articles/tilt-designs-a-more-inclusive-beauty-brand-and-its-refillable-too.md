If you’re wondering why more beauty brands haven’t created accessible packaging that everyone can use, you’re not alone. It’s enough to make anyone with a disability start their own makeup line.

Which is precisely what Aerin Glazer did.

Launching earlier this month, [Tilt Beauty](https://tiltbeauty.com/) is a brand whose foundation of accessibility and inclusivity puts ease and comfort front and center with each of their offerings. “Growing up with psoriatic arthritis, I struggled with beauty products that felt impossible to use while my friends enjoyed them effortlessly — that frustration sparked the idea for Tilt,” says founder Aerin Glazer on the company’s about page. “How could I create something accessible and covetable?” Glazer saw that most beauty products and their packaging weren’t ideal for those who experienced chronic pain, citing hacks like [grippy tape and rubber bands](https://newmobility.com/disabled-women-share-their-cosmetic-hacks/) for folks who have trouble opening and using everyday products.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/D_Designed_with_You_In_Mind.jpg.webp?resize=1040%2C1207&quality=65&ssl=1&_jb=custom)

Designed by [Established](https://establishednyc.com/), every touchpoint of the brand is highly intentional and considered, and they’ve more than earned the tagline of “beauty that feels better.” For starters, you’ll find braille and easy-pull tear strips on the outer boxes. They’ve also utilized the Braille Institute’s Atkinson Hyperlegible font on the boxes and the website to ensure overall readability for folks with low vision. The products themselves also use grippable cases. Lashscape’s mascara packaging features an oversized grip and a shortened wand that offers more control. Meanwhile, the Grip Stick Hydrating Lip Treatment sports a magnetic closure.

The ergonomic line has also resulted in Tilt being the first beauty brand to get a stamp of approval from The Arthritis Foundation, earning an Ease of Use certification for their patented packaging.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/Screenshot_2024-12-31_at_9.30.54_AM.png.webp?resize=350%2C399&quality=65&ssl=1&_jb=custom)

What’s more, everything Tilt makes is refillable, and according to the brand, you’ll reduce your plastic footprint by 76% when you forego buying a new mascara or hydrating lip treatment. Tilt has also partnered up with Pact Collective, so you can either throw your refill in one of their [drop-off bins](https://www.pactcollective.org/locations) or get a [free label](https://pact-collective.myshopify.com/products/tilt-beauty-x-pact-s-mail-back-collection-program?utm_source=copyToPasteBoard&utm_medium=product-links&utm_content=web) to mail it in so they can recycle it for you.

![](https://i0.wp.com/thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/Snapinst.app_476319449_17921143587020307_6588474904073500541_n_1080.jpg?resize=768%2C960&quality=70&ssl=1&_jb=closest)

Add it all up, and you have a brand setting new accessibility standards in the beauty industry, not to mention a lifesaver for those with extra sensitive skin.

Tilt is available exclusively at [tiltbeauty.com](https://tiltbeauty.com/)
